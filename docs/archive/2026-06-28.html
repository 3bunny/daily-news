This paper proposes 'Institutional Attestation' as a governance model for autonomous AI systems, particularly for high-stakes, irreversible actions like clinical prescribing or software deployment. Instead of attempting to monitor an AI's internal reasoning, which is complex and opaque, the model focuses on governing its actions. Under this framework, an AI retains autonomy in planning, but any designated high-risk action requires independent, cryptographically bound evidence (attestation) from separate authoritative sources, evaluated against a deterministic policy. All decisions are recorded in a tamper-evident log for independent re-verification. This model is crucial for AI safety and accountability as autonomous agents become more powerful. It offers a practical, auditable, and institutionally familiar approach to control AI by focusing on verifiable external actions rather than internal processes, fostering trust and enabling regulatory oversight.
