This significant expansion signals a strategic shift within the Pentagon, emphasizing the need for a much larger inventory of standoff munitions. The decision reflects a recognition of the potential for high-intensity conflicts, particularly against peer adversaries in contested environments like the Indo-Pacific. The LRASM, a derivative specifically designed for anti-ship warfare, is particularly crucial for countering advanced naval threats. The increased production also aligns with lessons from recent conflicts, which have highlighted the rapid consumption of munitions in modern warfare.
