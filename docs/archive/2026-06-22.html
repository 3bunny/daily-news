Read more
A notorious bot named "Jaredfromsubway.eth," responsible for a significant portion of "sandwich attacks" on the Ethereum blockchain, was recently exploited for $7.5 million. Sandwich attacks are a form of Maximal Extractable Value (MEV) exploitation, where an attacker identifies a pending transaction (e.g., a large cryptocurrency trade). They then place their own buy order immediately before the victim's transaction and a sell order immediately after, effectively "sandwiching" the victim's trade. This manipulation drives up the price for the victim and allows the attacker to profit from the price difference. This incident underscores the inherent risks and complex security landscape within decentralized finance (DeFi). Even sophisticated actors who profit from exploiting others are themselves vulnerable to attacks, highlighting the constant cat-and-mouse game in blockchain security. It also brings attention to the broader issue of MEV, a controversial practice where transaction order can be manipulated for profit, often at the expense of regular users. The exploitation of such a prominent bot serves as a stark reminder that no entity, regardless of its technical prowess, is entirely immune to the vulnerabilities present in the high-stakes world of blockchain.

The dot plot is a chart released quarterly by the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) that shows each member's projection for the federal funds rate at various points in the future. It serves as a form of forward guidance, helping markets understand the Fed's likely path for interest rates. This matters because financial markets rely on clear communication from central banks to price assets and make investment decisions.
