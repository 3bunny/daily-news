What Is Q-Day? The Quantum Threat to Bitcoin Explained

DecryptJul 04, 00:40 KST

Experts warn quantum computers could someday forge Bitcoin’s digital signatures, allowing unauthorized transactions.

Read more
"Q-Day" refers to a hypothetical future event where advanced quantum computers could potentially break the cryptographic algorithms that secure Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. Experts warn that sufficiently powerful quantum machines might be able to forge Bitcoin's digital signatures, thereby enabling unauthorized transactions and compromising the network's integrity. Bitcoin's security relies on elliptic curve cryptography (ECC), which is currently considered computationally infeasible to break with classical computers. However, quantum computers, utilizing algorithms like Shor's, could theoretically solve these complex mathematical problems much faster. While current quantum technology is not advanced enough to pose an immediate threat, the long-term concern is that future breakthroughs could undermine the foundational security of blockchain technology. Researchers are actively developing "post-quantum cryptography" to create new algorithms resistant to quantum attacks, which could eventually be integrated into blockchain protocols to mitigate this future risk.
